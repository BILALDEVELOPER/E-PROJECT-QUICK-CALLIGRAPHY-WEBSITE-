WESTERN CALLIGRAPHY

Western calligraphy is a visual art form that dates back to the early medieval period in Europe. It involves the design and execution of lettering with a broad-tipped instrument, brush, or other writing instrument. Known for its elaborate and intricate designs, Western calligraphy often features elaborate flourishes and decorative elements. Over the centuries, it has evolved from traditional scripts to contemporary artistic expressions.
